## 📊 What This Dashboard Does

Five interactive analysis modules, all controlled by a unified sidebar:

| Tab | What You Can Explore |
|-----|----------------------|
| **Price & Returns** | S&P 500 price history (linear/log), VIX fear index, drawdown from peak, return distribution vs normal fit, rolling Sharpe ratio |
| **Macro Factor Analysis** | Correlation heatmap, OLS regression (S&P 500 ~ ΔVIX + Δ10Y Yield + ΔUSD), macro time-series panel |
| **Sector Analysis** | Beta ranking for all 11 GICS sectors, annual returns heatmap, cumulative sector performance, rolling correlations |
| **Monte Carlo Simulation** | GBM simulation with user-adjustable horizon (63–504 days), simulation count, Normal vs Student-t distribution, market shock scenarios |
| **Risk Metrics** | VaR & CVaR at 90/95/99% confidence, rolling VaR over time, volatility regime analysis, top-10 worst trading days |

**Sidebar controls** (apply across all tabs simultaneously):
- Date range selector (2015 – present)
- VaR confidence level: 90% / 95% / 99%
- Rolling window: 1 month / 1 quarter / 6 months / 1 year
- Monte Carlo horizon and simulation count

## 🔬 Analytical Methods

- **Returns Engineering**: Log returns, simple returns, rolling volatility (21/63/252-day), rolling Sharpe ratio, maximum drawdown
- **Stationarity Testing**: Augmented Dickey-Fuller test on all regression inputs before OLS
- **OLS Regression**: S&P 500 returns ~ ΔVIX + Δ10Y Yield + ΔUSD (HC3 heteroscedasticity-robust standard errors)
- **Monte Carlo (GBM)**: Geometric Brownian Motion, 10,000 paths, user-adjustable horizon, Normal or Student-t innovations
- **Risk Metrics**: Historical VaR & CVaR (Expected Shortfall) at 90/95/99% confidence levels
- **Sector Beta**: OLS beta and alpha for all 11 GICS sectors vs S&P 500 benchmark

---

## 📈 Key Findings

- **CAGR: 11.03%** | Volatility: 17.88% | Sharpe: 0.435 | Max Drawdown: -33.92%
- **VIX is the dominant macro driver**: ΔVIX correlation with daily returns = **-0.73**. Each 1% VIX increase associates with ~0.25% daily equity decline
- **Fat tails confirmed**: Excess kurtosis = 15.81. CVaR (-2.76%) meaningfully exceeds VaR (-1.68%), validating Expected Shortfall as the more informative risk measure
- **Sector rotation**: Technology (XLK, β > 1.2) is most cyclically sensitive; Utilities (XLU) and Consumer Staples (XLP) are defensive (β < 0.7)
- **Diversification failure**: Sector correlations converge toward 1 during crisis periods (e.g. COVID-19 March 2020)
